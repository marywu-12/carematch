Our team is composed of 7 well-versed public health professionals. With experiences spanning the pharmaceutical industry, government sector, non-profit sector, medical practice sector, and global health initiatives, we aim to develop applicable solutions to empower underserved communities in need.

Cindy

Cindy is an MPH candidate in Public Health Policy, specializing in policy research. As a current employee at New York City DOHMH, she works on expanding health insurance access to marginalized communities in NYC. She has experience with real-time data at Broadstreet Institute, policy research at the E2GI lab, and scalable health solutions at the AGPHI lab.

Rhea Nakra

Rhea is an MPH candidate in Health Policy & Management, trained as a physician with experience in medical research, healthcare policy, and global health initiatives. She led the COVID-19 contact tracing team and, with 3 years as a Junior Doctor in Surgical Pathology, strengthened her clinical and analytic skills. As a Graduate Research Assistant, she collaborates with the UN-Habitat HER City project to design a digital platform empowering young girls in participatory urban planning.

Vickie (Vasiliki Glekas)

Vickie has 7 years of clinical experience in community pharmacy, alongside doctoral coursework in a PharmD program. She served as treasurer for a global non-profit (2022–2023) and is currently conducting research to develop an evidence-based intervention in the undergraduate NYU population.

Megana

Megana is a Research Assistant at the NYU Public Health Policy Lab, analyzing the effects of firearm restrictions in establishments that sell alcohol. She previously worked as a Behavior Technician implementing individualized behavioral plans for students with Autism Spectrum Disorder.

Christina Joseph

Christina is a researcher with experience in public health policy, urban health, and healthcare management. As a Volunteer Coordinator at Caregiver Sathi, she led 400 volunteers, managed databases, and raised awareness about caregiver challenges.

Sage Latella

Sage is an Outreach Coordinator focused on health promotion and community outreach, with research experience in infectious endocarditis treatment (2022–2025) and strong literature/scoping review skills. Sage has mentored women transitioning from foster care, volunteered with youth programs, and led teams as a student supervisor.
